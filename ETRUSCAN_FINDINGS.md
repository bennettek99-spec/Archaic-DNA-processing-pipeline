## D. Verdict
Genome-wide Neanderthal ancestry is essentially flat across the Italian transect (+0.0095 pp/kyr, p=0.76). No Etruscan individual is a significant archaic-ancestry outlier; crucially, the AADR steppe/Levantine/East-Mediterranean *genetic-ancestry* outliers are **not** archaic-ancestry outliers (mean |z| 0.45 vs 0.68 for typical Etruscans) — those alternative West-Eurasian ancestries carry similar ~2% Neanderthal, so the fine-scale ancestry variation in Iron-Age Etruria did **not** translate into archaic-ancestry differences. 1 of 11 introgression loci survive Bonferroni after controlling for ancestry (PCs) and overall archaic level: **FADS1-2** (p=1.2e-03, falling toward present, 5 archaic SNPs). These rest on few archaic-informative SNPs and are flagged as selection *candidates/hypotheses* for higher-coverage follow-up, not proof. FADS1-2 is a well-known target of strong dietary selection in Europeans (Mathieson 2015; Buckley 2017), so a temporal shift there is biologically plausible.
