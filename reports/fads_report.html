FADS1-2: a candidate Neanderthal-introgression selection signal

Deep-dive into the one locus flagged by the Etruscan/Italian scan · AADR 1240K · 2026-06-29
Exploratory. A hypothesis from 5 archaic-informative SNPs on capture data — not a validated selection scan.

Background

The FADS1/FADS2/FADS3 cluster (chr11q12-13) encodes fatty-acid desaturases for long-chain omega-3/6 synthesis. It is among the strongest recent-selection signals in Europeans — a regulatory haplotype boosting PUFA synthesis rose after the Neolithic dietary shift (Mathieson 2015; Ye 2017) — and carries adaptively introgressed archaic haplotypes in some populations (Buckley 2017).

Result

The FADS Neanderthal allele declines toward the present: Italy +6.679 pp/kyr (p=0.0000, n=406); Europe +6.343 pp/kyr (p=0.0000, n=5936). This indicates a pan-European pattern.

Interpretation

The five informative SNPs lie in the TMEM258–FADS1 regulatory block and include rs174537 and rs174550, canonical markers of the European FADS sweep and FADS1-expression eQTLs (Ameur 2012; Mathieson 2015). A declining archaic allele fits the post-Neolithic FADS sweep: the diet-adaptive haplotype that rose is the non-archaic modern-human regulatory haplotype, whose increase displaced the Neanderthal-matching alleles. Selection at FADS thus acted against the archaic background — the mirror image of classic adaptive-introgression loci (BNC2, OAS, TLR) where an archaic allele rose.

Crucially, the archaic allele is near fixation (~90%) in Mesolithic hunter-gatherers — far above a typical rare introgressed allele — so "archaic-informative" here largely captures common ancestral-haplotype variation (near-absent in Africans, carried at high frequency by pre-agricultural Europeans), not a recent Neanderthal-introgression event. The locus is best read as the canonical FADS dietary sweep, which our archaic-allele scan recovered independently and pan-continentally — not as Neanderthal adaptive introgression.

Caveats

Few archaic-informative SNPs on a capture array; FADS has a complex recombining haplotype structure; "archaic-informative" alleles can arise by incomplete lineage sorting or tag the modern selected haplotype by linkage; frequency change reflects selection and ancestry turnover (we control for genome-wide archaic ancestry and PC1). A definitive test needs haplotype-resolved data and an explicit time-series selection model.
